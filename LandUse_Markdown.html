Geting Trend from Existing Land Use in DC AREA

We decided to use the dataset of existing land use in the DC area. It records the land used for now and what the land used before. We can make a comparison and find out the interesting trend behind the redevelopment.

Source data: https://opendata.dc.gov/datasets/existing-land-use?geometry=-78.003%2C38.707%2C-76.026%2C39.081

Analysis steps 1.) Some columns we had no use for, so we decided to drop those from the dataset we were looking at. By comparing the land use at different times, we find that the undetermined lands are decreasing and used for different purposes. Most of the facilities lands tend to increase, including residential lands, commercial lands. In terms of redevelopment and gentrification this could speak to the rapid decrees of people in areas. The data shows once the people are moved out of these areas this causes the land to increase for residential housing or just growth of city limits. Our goal of our research is to determine what does gentrification look like and what are common trends within this analysis we will discover why.
